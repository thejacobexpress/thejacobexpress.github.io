Meet the leader.

As the vice president of the Tahoma Technology Student Association, I have facilitated high schoolers' passions of programming, 3D modelling, digital art, music technology, story writing, and more through the development of video games. The Tahoma TSA provides a space where students have access to guidance from experienced game designers, convenient and detailed online resources covering critical topics within game development, a treasury of funds to support the purchasing of cutting-edge video game development software, and other students who share their same passions! At the Tahoma TSA, students have the unique opportunity to create and innovate alongside fellow developers in an environment that allows them to push themselves to new heights.

Joining the Tahoma TSA as the vice president in my junior year, I was eager to dip my toes into every aspect of my first leadership position. I took every chance I could to present to the Tahoma TSA, advise members on video game development, and manage my teams' tasks for our game, "USG".

Stepping up to more responsibility and a larger vision for TSA in my senior year, I recruited a full executive team.

Alongside the new executive team, we provided the most support Tahoma TSA developers have ever had: an easily accessible archive of resources spanning across several video game development disciplines, the advisor system (a collection of skilled game designers within the TSA made easily accessible to all members through the Tahoma TSA Discord server and during in-person meetings), and capital supplied by the Tahoma TSA's fundraisers to fund the development of every team's game.

And I am always looking for more opportunities to improve.
